## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: What's the difference between a standard clean and a deep clean?
A: A **standard clean** focuses on routine maintenance: dusting accessible surfaces, vacuuming/mopping floors, basic kitchen/bathroom sanitization. A **deep clean**, however, is far more comprehensive. It includes detailed attention to areas often overlooked, such as baseboards, interior windows, door frames, light fixtures, scrubbing grout lines, and thorough appliance exteriors. Think of it as a reset for your home, often recommended for initial services or seasonal refreshers.

### Q: How often should I have my house professionally cleaned?
A: This largely depends on your lifestyle, household size, and specific needs. For active families, pet owners, or those with allergies, a **weekly** service often provides the most consistent benefit. **Bi-weekly** is a popular choice for many, maintaining a good level of cleanliness without constant upkeep. **Monthly** services are best for smaller households or those who handle some cleaning themselves but want periodic, professional attention to deeper tasks.

### Q: What professional certifications or affiliations should I look for in a cleaning company?
A: Look for affiliations with recognized industry bodies like the **ISSA (International Sanitary Supply Association)**. This demonstrates a commitment to professional standards, ongoing education, and best practices in cleaning and sanitization. While not always mandatory, it's a strong indicator of a company's dedication to quality and ethical operations. Some companies also emphasize training in specific areas like advanced floor care or proper use of hospital-grade disinfectants.

### Q: What kind of cleaning products and equipment do top-rated cleaners use?
A: High-quality professional cleaners prioritize efficacy and safety. Expect them to use **commercial-grade, concentrated cleaning agents** designed for specific tasks (e.g., degreasers, disinfectants, glass cleaners) that are effective at dilution. For equipment, look for **HEPA-filtered vacuums** that capture fine particulates and allergens, **microfiber cloths** for superior dirt and germ capture without scratching, and potentially **steam cleaners** for deep sanitization of hard surfaces or grout, utilizing a 275°F thermal shock for effective pathogen reduction. Avoid companies that rely solely on generic, all-purpose sprays.
